Events in Hackney

Hackney Carnival

Hackney Carnival
Hackney Carnival is basically Notting Hill’s smaller, more manageable little sister. The party officially runs from 11am until 7pm, with some sound systems kicking off around midday. The vibes won’t end at the official curfew though, so keep an eye out for official afterparties. There’ll also be food vendors all over Hackney Central and along the route to keep you well stocked on dancing fuel.

Oslo

Oslo
A new destination for food, drinks and music opens in Hackney, London. Drawing on a Nordic aesthetic, Oslo marks a significant new arrival in the dynamic and burgeoning area of Hackney Central, offering top quality food and drink alongside an exciting music programme in a unique space of a redeveloped railway station.

Hackney Empire

Hackney Empire
Today, it's a much-loved East End institution whose pantos have become the stuff of legend. High art does feature (the English Touring Opera presented Mozart's 'The Magic Flute' in 2009) as does issue-heavy theatre, often with an emphasis on class and multiculturalism. But the focus tends to be on fun: comedy, children's theatre and music all featuring large on its programme.

Hackney Museum

Hackney Museum
Less known than it should be, The Hackney Museum is a great example of diversity among the local community as many of its exhibits tell the story of how people from all over the world have made London their home. Star exhibits include the ‘Hackney Hoard’, a collection of double eagle coins unearthed in a Hackney garden, a propaganda sheet from Nazi Germany and a sculpture made by a child from Sierra Leone.

Hackney Picturehouse

Hackney Picturehouse
These days it's impossible to imagine Hackney without this four-screen branch of the Picturehouse chain, opened in 2011. The buzzy ground-floor bar/café serves good burgers, and bang-on programming mixes top-of-the-range mainstream with artier films. On the top floor, the Hackney Attic is home to music quizzes, open-mic nights and other live events.
